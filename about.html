Why I became an ADHD coach

[This section is where you share your personal story or connection to ADHD coaching. For example: your own experience of ADHD, a family member's diagnosis, or a professional journey that brought you here.]

What I know from both experience and training is that ADHD is not a character flaw, a lack of effort, or something to be "fixed." It is a different kind of brain — one that can struggle enormously in systems not built for it, but that also brings real strengths, creativity, and depth.

Coaching gave me [or: people I care about / the clients I've worked with] something that felt genuinely different: a space to understand, to problem-solve without shame, and to build strategies that actually stick. That is what I want to offer through Puzzle Peace.

The name Puzzle Peace came from the idea of finding peace — not by forcing all the puzzle pieces to fit the way they "should," but by understanding how your own unique picture fits together.
