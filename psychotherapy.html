Psychotherapy

A space to explore, make sense of your experience, and find your way forward.

One-to-one person-centred psychotherapy

Psychotherapy at Puzzle Peace is one-to-one, open-ended, and led entirely by you. Unlike coaching — which is about building strategies and moving forward — therapy is a space to slow down, look inward, and understand the experiences and patterns that have shaped who you are.

The approach is built on the belief that, given the right conditions — genuine warmth, empathy, and a space free from judgement — people naturally move towards growth and healing. My role is not to direct or advise, but to be fully present with you, and to help you feel genuinely heard. The therapeutic relationship is not just the container for the work. It is the work.

Session format

Each session lasts one hour and takes place in person at my practice in NG11, or online via Zoom. My practice is quiet, comfortable, and easy to get to.

There is no set structure or agenda to each session. You bring whatever is on your mind that day — and we follow that.

Booking sessions

Psychotherapy sessions are booked on a session-by-session basis — there is no requirement to commit to a block up front.

Sessions are typically arranged once a week or once a fortnight, depending on what feels right for you. We will naturally review how things are going as we work together.

Payment

Payment is required in advance for each session. This secures your time in the diary. Payment details will be confirmed when you book.

I accept bank transfer or cash. If you have a question about payment, please do get in touch.

What might we explore?

Psychotherapy can be helpful for a wide range of experiences. Here are some of the areas people often bring to sessions — though you do not need to identify with any of them to start.

🌱

Past experiences

Understanding how earlier experiences have shaped the way you think, feel, and relate to yourself and others today.

💞

Relationships

Exploring patterns in how you connect with others — in friendships, family, or work — and what might be getting in the way.

🌊

Anxiety & worry

Making sense of what is driving your anxiety, and finding a different relationship with the thoughts and feelings that keep you stuck.

🌙

Low mood

Exploring what is underneath low mood, and gently working towards a greater sense of meaning, energy, and self-compassion.

🪞

Sense of self

Questions of identity, self-worth, and how you see and value yourself — and why it can be so difficult to feel secure in your own skin.

🌀

Life transitions

Navigating change, loss, endings, and the uncertainty of what comes next — and finding your footing again.

What to expect

The first session is a chance to get to know each other and for you to say something about what has brought you here. There is no set structure — just a conversation, at your pace, about whatever feels most important to say first. Many people find it comes as something of a relief.

  • There is no homework or set agenda — you bring whatever is on your mind.
  • Therapy can feel slow at times. That is normal, and often where the most meaningful shifts happen.
  • Trust and rapport take time — that is part of the process, not a delay before it starts.
  • You do not need to arrive with a clear problem. Some people come simply wanting to understand themselves better.
  • Everything you share is confidential (see my Privacy Policy for the small number of exceptions).

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to have a specific problem to start therapy?

No. Some people come to therapy with a clear difficulty they want to work through. Others come because something feels off and they are not quite sure what. Both are entirely valid starting points — you do not need to have the words for it yet.

How long will I need therapy?

There is no fixed answer. Some people find that a handful of sessions gives them what they need; for others, therapy is something they return to over a longer period. We will naturally review how things feel as we go along, and you are always free to take a break or end whenever you are ready.

Is psychotherapy the same as ADHD coaching?

No — they are quite different. Coaching is a practical, forward-focused partnership built around strategies and goals. Psychotherapy is more exploratory: it is about understanding your experience and making sense of patterns, and the therapeutic relationship itself is part of how change happens. If you are unsure which might suit you better, the free call is a good place to start, or you can read more on the Services page.

Where do sessions take place?

Sessions are in person at [location], or online via Zoom. If you have accessibility needs or travel difficulties, please do mention this when you get in touch — I am happy to have a conversation about how we can make it work.

How do I know if psychotherapy is right for me?

If you find yourself going around in circles with the same thoughts or patterns — or if something from the past still feels like it is getting in the way — psychotherapy might be worth exploring. The free call is a low-pressure place to have that conversation. I will always be honest with you if I think a different kind of support might serve you better.
